Kashmir Seasons: Overview

Kashmir's four distinct seasons each offer a completely different travel experience. Quick Answer: September–October and May deliver the best balance of weather, crowd levels, price, and full accessibility for most travellers. But each season has compelling reasons to visit.

📍 Regional Weather Variations

  • Srinagar (Valley, 1,587 m): Summer 20–28°C · Winter -2 to 3°C
  • Gulmarg (2,650 m): Summer 12–18°C · Winter -10 to -5°C
  • Pahalgam (2,130 m): Summer 15–22°C · Winter -2 to 5°C
  • Sonmarg (2,800 m): Summer 10–18°C · Road closed Nov–May
  • Anantnag (1,700 m): Warmest region, accessible year-round

Spring in Kashmir — March to May 🌸

Spring transforms Kashmir. Almond and cherry orchards burst into blossom in March. By April the Valley floor is carpeted in wildflowers. May brings peak bloom in the alpine meadows. Temperatures range 5–25°C — comfortable without summer heat or humidity. Shoulder-season prices apply.

March: 5–15°C · Occasional snow at altitude · Good for budget travellers · Lower crowds · Tulip Festival preparation begins

April: 10–18°C · Tulip Garden peak bloom (Indira Gandhi Tulip Garden opens late March–mid April) · Wildflowers at lower elevations · Good trekking

May ⭐: 15–25°C · Perfect conditions · All regions accessible · Gulmarg meadows in full bloom · Recommended for families, couples, first-time visitors

Summer in Kashmir — June to August ☀️

Kashmir's peak tourist season with warm temperatures (18–28°C), long days (up to 15 hrs daylight), and every attraction at full operation. Expect the highest prices and largest crowds — book well in advance.

June: 18–24°C · School holidays begin · All water activities running · Crowds increasing

July: 20–26°C · Peak of tourist season · Amarnath Yatra in progress · Humidity highest · Book 2–3 months ahead for popular properties

August: 18–24°C · Late summer · Lotus blooms cover Manasbal Lake · Crowds begin easing late August

Autumn in Kashmir — September to October ⭐⭐ TOP PICK

Autumn is our strongest recommendation for most travellers. The monsoon retreats, the sky becomes a brilliant blue canvas, mountain views are at their sharpest, and Chinar trees begin their spectacular transition to red and gold (peak: 15–30 October).

September ⭐⭐: 15–25°C · Crystal clarity · Crowds drop from August peak · Prices moderate · Best overall combination for all activities · Our single top recommendation

October ⭐: 8–20°C · Chinar foliage peaks mid–late month · Photography at its finest · Cooler evenings — bring a jacket · Last good month before winter sets in

Winter in Kashmir — November to February ❄️

Winter delivers a snow-globe Kashmir — dramatically beautiful but demanding preparation. Gulmarg becomes India's finest ski resort (December–February). Dal Lake partially freezes in harsh winters.

November: 5–15°C (early) to 0–8°C (late) · Rapid temperature drop · Good transitional month for budget travellers · Some high-altitude roads close from mid-November

December–February: -2 to 5°C (Srinagar) · -10 to -2°C (Gulmarg) · Heavy snowfall · Gulmarg skiing season at its world-class best · Lowest accommodation prices · Some hotels closed

Festivals & Events Calendar

  • March–April: Tulip Festival, Srinagar (Asia's largest tulip garden in bloom)
  • May–September: Kashmir Summer Festival (music, dance, cultural events)
  • June–August: Amarnath Yatra pilgrimage season
  • September: Lal Ded Festival (Kashmiri literature & poetry)
  • October: Peak Chinar foliage season
  • December–February: Winter Sports & Skiing Season (Gulmarg)
  • Variable (Islamic calendar): Eid-ul-Fitr, Eid-ul-Adha — vibrant local celebrations

What to Pack — Season by Season

Summer (May–August)

  • Light cotton layers · Light jacket for high-altitude evenings
  • Rain jacket (July–August showers) · Comfortable walking shoes
  • SPF 50+ sunscreen · Sunglasses · Hat · Insect repellent (lake areas)

Autumn (September–October)

  • Medium-weight sweater or fleece · Light jacket · Windproof layer
  • Long trousers · Comfortable trekking shoes · Sunscreen & sunglasses

Winter (November–February)

  • Down jacket or heavy wool coat · Thermal inner layers · Waterproof snow boots
  • Woollen hat · Gloves · Thick socks · Hand and toe warmers
  • Rich moisturiser & SPF lip balm (cold dry air)
