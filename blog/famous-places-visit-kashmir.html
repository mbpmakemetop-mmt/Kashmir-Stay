Kashmir's Top Tourist Destinations — Overview

Kashmir, the "Paradise on Earth," offers some of India's most breathtaking natural landscapes, sacred pilgrimage sites, Mughal heritage, and frontier adventure destinations. This guide ranks 50 famous places based on popularity, accessibility, unique experience, and tourist ratings — helping you plan any length of trip from a 5-day city break to a 14-day adventure expedition.

🎯 Planning Quick Facts

  • Best time: May–September (all roads open, pleasant weather)
  • Recommended duration: 10–14 days for comprehensive coverage
  • Base city: Srinagar (most attractions within 2–3 hours)
  • Budget (estimated): ₹2,000–5,000/day budget · ₹5,000–15,000 mid-range · ₹15,000+ luxury — verify all rates on KashmirStay.in
  • Transport: Hire private taxi or join verified tours (₹2,000–4,000/day)

🏆 Top 10 Must-Visit Famous Places in Kashmir

🥇 #1 — Dal Lake, Srinagar

Popularity: 9.8/10 · Year-Round Destination

The most iconic Kashmir destination. Shikara rides through floating gardens, houseboat overnight stays, the famous floating vegetable market at dawn, Char Chinar island, and Nehru Park. No Kashmir trip is complete without Dal Lake.

Activities: Shikara rides · Houseboat stay · Floating market · Photography · Walking Boulevard Road

Distance from airport: 12 km · Entry: Free · Read full Dal Lake guide →

🥈 #2 — Gulmarg · Meadow of Flowers

Popularity: 9.7/10 · 52 km from Srinagar · 2,650 m altitude

India's premier ski resort and home to one of the world's highest cable car systems. Gulmarg Gondola rises to 3,979 m for panoramic views of Nanga Parbat and the full Kashmir Valley. Summer wildflower meadows are equally spectacular.

Activities: Gondola ride · Skiing (Dec–Mar) · Trekking · Golf · Wildflower walks

🥉 #3 — Shalimar Bagh · Abode of Love

Popularity: 9.5/10 · 14 km from Srinagar centre

Built by Emperor Jahangir in 1619 for Nur Jahan. 31 acres of terraced gardens with 410 fountains, 584 ancient Chinar trees, and evening illuminated fountain shows. The grandest of Srinagar's Mughal gardens.

Entry (est.): ₹50–100 · Timings: 9 AM – 7 PM

#4 — Nishat Bagh · Garden of Joy

Popularity: 9.4/10 · 18 km from Srinagar centre

Kashmir's largest Mughal garden (1633), with 12 terraces representing the zodiac on Dal Lake's eastern shore. Stunning sunset views. Spring flower display (Mar–May) unmatched.

#5 — Pahalgam · Valley of Shepherds

Popularity: 9.2/10 · 96 km from Srinagar · 2,130 m altitude

Gateway to Amarnath Yatra pilgrimage, base for high-altitude treks, and Bollywood's favourite filming location. Betaab Valley, Aru Valley, Chandanwari and Baisaran (Mini Switzerland) are sub-valley highlights.

#6 — Sonmarg · Meadow of Gold

Popularity: 8.9/10 · 87 km from Srinagar · 2,800 m altitude

Gateway to Ladakh via Zoji La Pass. Thajiwas Glacier accessible by pony or trek. Sindh River camping, multi-day alpine lake treks (Gangabal, Vishansar). Road open May–October only.

#7 — Betaab Valley (Pahalgam)

Popularity: 8.7/10 · 110 km from Srinagar · 14 km from Pahalgam

Named after the 1983 Bollywood film "Betaab," this picturesque valley surrounded by snow-capped peaks, dense forest and a crystal-clear stream is one of Kashmir's most photogenic locations.

#8 — Aru Valley (Pahalgam)

Popularity: 8.5/10 · 6 km from Pahalgam

Starting point for Kolahoi Glacier trek and Tarsar-Marsar lake trek. Less crowded than Betaab Valley. Pristine meadows, dense pine forest. Free entry.

#9 — Shankaracharya Temple · Jyotirling

Popularity: 8.4/10 · 1–2 km from Srinagar centre · 1,100 m altitude

One of India's oldest temples (1st century CE) sits atop a hill with 360° panoramic views of Srinagar and Dal Lake. Sacred pilgrimage site and best sunrise viewpoint in the city.

#10 — Wular Lake · Asia's Largest Freshwater Lake

Popularity: 8.3/10 · 65 km from Srinagar

Spanning up to 212 km², Wular Lake is a Ramsar-listed wetland supporting 200+ bird species and rich fisheries. Spectacular sunrises, boat rides, and birding November–March when migratory species arrive.

🏙️ Srinagar City Attractions — Rank 11–15

  • #11 — Chashme Shahi Garden (1632): Intimate Mughal garden built around a medicinal natural spring. 12 km from centre. Est. ₹50 entry.
  • #12 — Jama Masjid Srinagar (1402): Historic mosque with 370 wooden pillars. Old City Srinagar. Free entry. 1 hr visit.
  • #13 — Hazratbal Shrine: Sacred Islamic site housing a hair relic, white marble mosque on Dal Lake northern shore. Free entry.
  • #14 — Floating Vegetable Market: Dal Lake dawn spectacle (5–7 AM). Access by shikara from Dal Gate (est. ₹800–1,000).
  • #15 — Indira Gandhi Memorial Tulip Garden: Asia's largest tulip garden (30 acres, 1.5 million bulbs). Open late March–mid April only. Est. ₹50–100 entry.

🚠 Gulmarg & North Kashmir — Rank 16–25

  • #16 — Gulmarg Gondola: Phase 1 (2,650–3,080 m) + Phase 2 (to 3,979 m Apharwat Peak). Est. ₹800–1,600 per person — verify current rates.
  • #17 — Yusmarg Meadows (47 km): Hidden meadow at 2,396 m. Dense cedar forests, pony rides, camping. Minimal commercialisation.
  • #18 — Doodhpathri (42 km): "Valley of Milk" meadow at 2,730 m. Milky white streams, Gujjar shepherds, no commercialisation. Day trip from Srinagar.
  • #19 — Aharbal Waterfall: 40 m seasonal waterfall 75 km from Srinagar. Scenic trekking approach. Free entry.
  • #20 — Khilanmarg (Gulmarg): 5 km from Gulmarg, panoramic meadow for picnics, camping and photography. Accessible on foot or by pony.
  • #21–25: Alpather Lake Trek · Tangmarg Village Orchards · Lolab Valley (Kupwara) · Bandipora Markets · Wular Lake Birdwatching

🏞️ Pahalgam & South Kashmir — Rank 26–35

  • #26 — Lidder River (Pahalgam): Trout fishing, rafting (April–June), riverside camping. Fishing permit from Fisheries Department required.
  • #27 — Kolahoi Glacier Trek: 18 km from Aru Valley base camp. Moderate-challenging. 2 days. Guide recommended.
  • #28 — Baisaran (Mini Switzerland): 5 km trek or pony ride from Pahalgam (est. ₹800–1,000). Vast green meadows resembling Swiss Alps.
  • #29 — Achabal Garden: Mughal garden with powerful natural spring, 58 km from Srinagar. Est. ₹50 entry.
  • #30 — Martand Sun Temple Ruins: 8th-century Hindu temple ruins near Anantnag. Free entry. Archaeologically significant.
  • #31–35: Chandanwari · Anchar Lake · Daksum · Verinag Spring Garden · Saffron Fields Pampore (harvest Oct–Nov)

⛰️ Sonmarg & Adventure — Rank 36–45

  • #36 — Thajiwas Glacier (Sonmarg): Pony (est. ₹1,200–1,600 return) or 2–3 hr trek. Snow year-round.
  • #37 — Zoji La Pass (3,528 m): 18 km from Sonmarg. Gateway to Ladakh via Srinagar–Leh Highway. Thrilling drive.
  • #38 — Vishansar & Krishansar Lake Trek: 4–5 day alpine lakes trek from Sonmarg. Moderate-high difficulty. Guide essential.
  • #39 — Gurez Valley: Remote pristine valley 6–7 hrs from Srinagar. Habba Khatoon Peak, Kishanganga River, Dard Shin culture. Permit may be required. Read Gurez guide →
  • #40 — Dachigam National Park (22 km): Hangul (Kashmir Stag) reserve. Guided tours only. Entry permit required — est. ₹400–600.
  • #41–45: Sindh River Camping · Bangus Valley (permit req.) · Manasbal Lake Lotus Bloom · Sinthan Top Pass · Gangabal Lake Trek

💎 Hidden Gems & Offbeat — Rank 46–50

  • #46 — Lidderwat Alpine Camp: Remote camping spot from Pahalgam. 2–3 hr trek. Horse access. Pristine, uncrowded.
  • #47 — Saffron Fields of Pampore: Kashmir's saffron heartland, 30 km from Srinagar. Farm tours available (est. ₹200–500). Harvest: October–November.
  • #48 — Tulip Garden Bloom (seasonal): Open only late March–mid April. 68 tulip varieties, 1.5 million bulbs. Asia's largest. Est. ₹50–100 entry.
  • #49 — Old City Srinagar Heritage Walk: Ancient bazaar, carved wooden architecture, old mosques, traditional craft workshops. Free, guide optional (est. ₹500).
  • #50 — Nigeen Lake: Dal Lake's quieter, cleaner neighbour. Water-skiing, luxury houseboats, peaceful atmosphere. Less crowded than Dal.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What's the best time to visit Kashmir's famous places?

A: May–September is ideal — all roads open, pleasant weather. September–October offers best clarity with lower crowds. Winter (Dec–Feb) for skiing. See our complete month-by-month guide.

Q: Is Kashmir safe for tourists?

A: Kashmir's major tourist destinations are generally safe. Millions visit annually without incidents. Follow government advisories, use registered operators from Kashmir Stay's verified directory, and avoid areas near the Line of Control without proper permits.

Q: Do foreigners need permits for Kashmir?

A: Foreign nationals don't typically need permits for major tourist areas. Remote border areas (Gurez, Bangus) may require inner-line permits — apply through J&K Tourism Department 2–3 weeks in advance.

Q: Are there vegetarian food options in Kashmir?

A: Yes. While traditional Wazwan is meat-centric, vegetarian restaurants are common in Srinagar and tourist towns. Dum Aloo, Nadru Yakhni (lotus stem), Haak (greens), and Modur Pulao (sweet saffron rice) are classic Kashmiri vegetarian dishes.
