Sinthan Top: Distances From Srinagar, Pahalgam, Anantnag, Daksum, Jammu & Sonmarg

Sinthan Top (also written as Sinthan Pass or Sinthan Top Pass) is one of the highest motorable mountain passes in Anantnag district, Kashmir, sitting at an elevation of around 12,000 feet (3,658 meters). It connects the Kashmir Valley with the Kishtwar region of Jammu through a stunning alpine landscape of snow-capped peaks, meadows, and pine forests. Travellers from Srinagar, Pahalgam, Anantnag, Daksum, Jammu, and Sonmarg regularly visit Sinthan Top for snow activities, photography, and scenic road trips. Srinagar to Sinthan Top Distance

The distance from Srinagar to Sinthan Top is approximately 120 km, and the drive takes around 4 to 5 hours depending on road conditions and stops along the way. This is one of the most searched routes for "sinthan top distance from srinagar" because it forms the gateway journey for most tourists heading from the summer capital towards this high-altitude pass.

The route from Srinagar runs along the National Highway towards Anantnag, then branches off through Achabal and Daksum onto the Sinthan Pass road. This stretch is one of Kashmir's most scenic mountain drives, passing through pine forests, alpine meadows, and the upper reaches of the Bringi valley before reaching the snow-clad Sinthan Top.

Pahalgam to Sinthan Top Distance

The distance from Pahalgam to Sinthan Top is approximately 75-80 km, with a driving time of around 2.5 to 3 hours. Many tourists staying in Pahalgam plan a day trip to Sinthan Top because it offers a completely different alpine experience compared to Pahalgam's meadows.

Pahalgam to Sinthan Top Route

  • Pahalgam to Anantnag: ~45 km (around 1 - 1.25 hours) along the connecting road back to the main highway
  • Anantnag to Daksum: ~25 km (45 minutes to 1 hour) through Achabal and orchard country
  • Daksum to Sinthan Top: ~25-30 km (1 - 1.5 hours) on the winding mountain pass road

This route is popular for travellers who want to combine Pahalgam's meadows with the high-altitude snow point at Sinthan Top in a single day trip, though an early start is recommended to allow enough time at the top and a comfortable return before evening.

Anantnag to Sinthan Top Distance

The distance from Anantnag to Sinthan Top is approximately 60-65 km, taking around 2 to 2.5 hours of mountain driving. Anantnag is the gateway town for the Sinthan Pass road, and most distance calculations for Srinagar, Pahalgam, and Jammu routes pass through this town.

Anantnag to Sinthan Top Route

  • Anantnag to Achabal: ~10 km (20-30 minutes), passing the famous Achabal Mughal Gardens
  • Achabal to Daksum: ~20-25 km (40-50 minutes) through villages and forest edges
  • Daksum to Sinthan Top: ~25-30 km (1 - 1.5 hours) on the high-altitude pass road with multiple hairpin bends

This stretch climbs steadily from Anantnag's orchard valleys (around 5,400 feet) to Sinthan Top's high alpine zone (around 12,000 feet), making it a dramatic transition in landscape and temperature within a relatively short distance.

Daksum to Sinthan Top Distance

The distance from Daksum to Sinthan Top is approximately 25-30 km, with a travel time of about 1 to 1.5 hours. Daksum is the last major settlement before the climb to Sinthan Top and is a popular base camp for travellers wanting to visit the pass early in the morning.

Why Daksum is Important for Sinthan Top Trips

  • Last fuel and food stop: Daksum has small dhabas and shops before the climb begins
  • Forest rest house: Basic accommodation available for an overnight halt
  • Scenic riverside location: Surrounded by deodar and pine forests along the Bringi river
  • Gateway to the climb: The road becomes steep and winding immediately after Daksum, climbing rapidly toward the snowline

Jammu to Sinthan Top Distance

The distance from Jammu to Sinthan Top is approximately 280-300 km, with a total travel time of around 8 to 9 hours including a halt. This route is mainly used by tourists arriving from outside Kashmir who plan to combine the Jammu-Srinagar highway journey with a detour to Sinthan Top.

Sonmarg to Sinthan Top Distance

The distance from Sonmarg to Sinthan Top is approximately 100-110 km, taking around 3.5 to 4 hours via Srinagar and Anantnag. Since both Sonmarg and Sinthan Top are high-altitude scenic points but located on opposite sides of the valley, this route involves coming back down to the Srinagar-Anantnag highway before climbing again towards Daksum and the Sinthan Pass.

Sonmarg to Sinthan Top Route

  • Sonmarg to Srinagar: ~80 km (2 - 2.5 hours) back along the Srinagar-Leh highway
  • Srinagar to Anantnag: ~55 km (1.5 hours) via the main highway
  • Anantnag to Sinthan Top: ~60-65 km (2 - 2.5 hours) via Daksum

Due to the distance and multiple high-altitude stretches involved, travellers combining Sonmarg and Sinthan Top in one trip should plan for an early start and ideally treat it as a multi-day Kashmir itinerary covering Srinagar, Sonmarg, Pahalgam, and Sinthan Top across separate days.

About Sinthan Top: Kashmir's High-Altitude Pass

Sinthan Top (also called Sinthan Pass) is a high mountain pass located in Anantnag district, Jammu and Kashmir, at an elevation of approximately 12,000 feet (3,658 meters) above sea level. It connects the Kashmir Valley with the Kishtwar district of the Jammu region, making it an important link between the two regions as well as a major tourist attraction in its own right.

Why Sinthan Top is Popular

  • Snow Point: One of the most accessible high-altitude snow points near Anantnag, often retaining snow well into summer
  • Panoramic Views: 360-degree views of snow-capped peaks, alpine meadows, and the Kishtwar mountain ranges
  • Day Trip Destination: Easily combined with Pahalgam or Daksum for a day excursion
  • Photography & Filming: Dramatic landscapes make it popular for photography, videography, and film shoots
  • Adventure Driving: The winding mountain pass road is itself a major attraction for road-trip enthusiasts
  • Less Crowded: Compared to Gulmarg or Sonmarg, Sinthan Top sees fewer tourists, offering a quieter experience

Sinthan Top Road Conditions

  • Srinagar to Anantnag: Wide national highway, generally well-maintained
  • Anantnag to Daksum: Good two-lane road through villages and orchards
  • Daksum to Sinthan Top: Narrow, winding mountain road with multiple hairpin bends; can be snow-covered or muddy depending on season
  • Vehicle Recommendation: SUVs or vehicles with good ground clearance are recommended for the final stretch, especially in early season when snow may still be present

⚠️ Important Travel Notes for Sinthan Top

Seasonal Closure: Sinthan Top often remains closed or partially inaccessible during heavy snow months (December to April). Always check current road status before travelling.

Limited Facilities: Food and fuel options are very limited beyond Daksum, so carry adequate supplies, water, and warm clothing.

Start Early: For day trips from Srinagar or Pahalgam, an early morning start is essential to allow time at Sinthan Top and a safe return before dark.

Local Guidance: Road conditions can change quickly due to weather; it is advisable to check with local drivers or tour operators before the final ascent.
