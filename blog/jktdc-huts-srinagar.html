What is JKTDC? — J&K Tourism Development Corporation

The Jammu & Kashmir Tourism Development Corporation (JKTDC) — officially operating under the Department of Tourism, J&K Government — is the government body responsible for developing, managing, and promoting tourism infrastructure across the Union Territory of Jammu & Kashmir. Established to make tourism accessible to all income groups, JKTDC operates a network of budget-friendly tourist bungalows, huts, log cabins, tourist homes, and resorts at key tourist destinations throughout J&K.

JKTDC properties are government-regulated, meaning their tariffs are approved by the Tourism Department and are significantly lower than comparable private hotels. They serve as reliable, safe, and affordable accommodation options particularly popular with domestic travellers, pilgrim groups (Amarnath and Vaishno Devi yatris), government servants travelling on official duty, and budget-conscious foreign backpackers who want a clean base without paying premium prices.

In Srinagar, the primary gateway for JKTDC bookings is the Tourist Reception Centre (TRC) on Boulevard Road — an iconic landmark that has served as Kashmir's central tourism hub for decades. The TRC not only houses accommodation units but also functions as the central booking and information office for all JKTDC properties across J&K.

📌 Key Fact: JKTDC operates over 80 tourist bungalows, huts, and log cabins across Jammu & Kashmir — covering major destinations like Srinagar, Gulmarg, Pahalgam, Sonamarg, Yusmarg, Doodhpathri, Gurez Valley, and dozens of other scenic and pilgrimage locations.

🏨 JKTDC Properties in Srinagar — Full List

Srinagar has the highest concentration of JKTDC properties in Kashmir. These are primarily clustered around the Dal Lake Boulevard, the Tourist Reception Centre, and the city's main tourist neighbourhoods. Here is a detailed look at the major JKTDC accommodation units in Srinagar:

🏛 Tourist Reception Centre (TRC) — Srinagar

The Tourist Reception Centre (TRC) is the flagship JKTDC complex in Srinagar — and arguably the most important tourism facility in all of Kashmir. Located on Boulevard Road along the banks of Dal Lake, TRC is simultaneously a hotel, information centre, booking office, bus terminus (JKSRTC and private bus services), and taxi/cab booking hub.

🏛
Tourist Reception Centre (TRC), Srinagar

The TRC complex in Srinagar functions as both the nerve centre of J&K Tourism and a fully operational tourist accommodation facility. The TRC accommodation wing offers clean rooms and dormitory beds at government-regulated prices, making it one of the most affordable stays near Dal Lake in Srinagar. The complex includes a reception hall, cafeteria, tourist information counters, and a JKTDC booking desk from which travellers can reserve accommodation at any JKTDC property across Kashmir.

The TRC is also the departure and arrival point for JKSRTC (government) buses to Jammu, Delhi, Leh, Kargil, and within Kashmir — making it an enormously practical stay for travellers arriving or departing by bus. The Dal Lake is virtually across the road, and the main houseboat ghats are a 5-minute walk away.

🗺 JKTDC Huts at Other Kashmir Locations

While this guide focuses on Srinagar, JKTDC operates huts and bungalows across the entire Kashmir Valley. Here is a brief overview of JKTDC properties at other major tourist destinations:

JKTDC Pahalgam

JKTDC operates several properties in Pahalgam — including wooden log huts along the Lidder River, tourist bungalows near the main bazaar, and tent camps at Chandanwari (seasonal, May–September). The Pahalgam JKTDC properties are excellent bases for trekking in Aru Valley and visiting Betab Valley. Rates range from ₹1,000 to ₹3,500/night.

JKTDC Gulmarg

In Gulmarg, JKTDC maintains a tourist bungalow and a small hut complex near the main meadow. These fill up quickly in ski season (December–March) and during peak summer. Advance booking through the Srinagar TRC or jktourism.gov.in is essential. Rates: ₹1,500–₹4,000/night.

JKTDC Sonamarg

Sonamarg has JKTDC tent camps and a tourist bungalow close to the Thajiwas Glacier trailhead. These are seasonal (open May–October only) and offer excellent value for trekkers and glacier-day-trippers. The government camp here is one of the most scenic JKTDC stays in Kashmir. Rates: ₹800–₹2,500/night / ₹500/bed (tent dormitory).

JKTDC Yusmarg

Yusmarg has a well-regarded JKTDC tourist bungalow near the meadow — one of the more spacious government properties in the valley. It includes a small restaurant and parking, and is often combined with Doodhpathri as a 2-night off-beaten-path Kashmir circuit. Rates: ₹900–₹2,200/night.

JKTDC Doodhpathri

JKTDC maintains a basic but scenic campsite and rest house at Doodhpathri. Given the remote location, this is one of the more adventurous JKTDC stays — the lack of mobile network and commercial activity creates a genuine wilderness atmosphere. Generator power only; meals arranged by the camp cook. Rates: ₹700–₹1,500/night.

JKTDC Gurez Valley

A JKTDC tourist bungalow operates in Dawar, the main village of Gurez Valley. Given the remoteness of Gurez (Inner Line Permit required), the JKTDC property here is the most reliable and recommended stay — private accommodation options are extremely limited. Rates: ₹800–₹1,800/night. Booking well in advance is strongly advised.

📋 How to Book JKTDC Huts in Srinagar — Step by Step

Booking JKTDC accommodation has become significantly easier in recent years, with online booking available for most properties. Here are all the ways to book:

Method 1 — Online Booking (Recommended)

  1. Visit the official J&K Tourism website: jktourism.gov.in
  2. Navigate to "Tourist Bungalows & Accommodation" or click the online booking portal
  3. Select your destination (Srinagar, Pahalgam, Gulmarg, etc.) and property type
  4. Choose check-in and check-out dates and select room category
  5. Fill in guest details (name, phone, ID proof number)
  6. Pay online via UPI, credit/debit card, or net banking
  7. Save your booking confirmation (PDF) — take a printout or screenshot

Method 2 — In-Person at TRC Srinagar

  1. Visit the JKTDC Booking Counter at Tourist Reception Centre (TRC), Boulevard Road, Srinagar
  2. Request booking form for the desired property and dates
  3. Submit valid ID proof (Aadhaar, Voter ID, Passport, or Driving Licence)
  4. Pay in cash or UPI at the counter
  5. Collect your booking receipt — present it on arrival at the property

Method 3 — Phone Booking

Call the TRC Srinagar booking line at 0194-2452691 during office hours (Mon–Sat, 10 AM – 5 PM) to check availability and make provisional bookings. Payment is required in advance or upon arrival depending on availability.

⚠️ Important: During peak season (May–September) and major festivals/yatras, JKTDC properties fill up rapidly — sometimes weeks in advance. Always book at least 2–3 weeks ahead for summer visits. Walk-in availability cannot be guaranteed during July–August.
💡 Pro Tip: When booking at the TRC counter in person, you can request to see photographs of available rooms before confirming. JKTDC staff will generally accommodate this and can also advise on the best-value properties for your requirements.

🛏 What to Expect — JKTDC Amenities & Facilities

JKTDC accommodation is clean, safe, and functional — but it is government-run, so the experience differs from a private boutique hotel. Here is an honest rundown of what to expect:

Standard Amenities at Most JKTDC Properties

  • Clean rooms with attached bathrooms (most properties)
  • Hot water supply via geyser (electric) — reliable at most Srinagar properties
  • Room heaters available in winter (small surcharge may apply)
  • Basic furniture: bed, table, chair, wardrobe, mirror
  • On-site restaurant or canteen at larger properties (basic Kashmiri and North Indian menu)
  • 24-hour security and front desk service at TRC and major bungalows
  • Parking available at most JKTDC properties
  • Wi-Fi — increasingly available, especially at TRC and Nishat Bungalow (may be inconsistent)

What JKTDC Properties Do NOT Generally Offer

  • Luxury furnishings, high-thread-count linen, or 5-star finishes
  • Swimming pools, gyms, or spa facilities (except at JKTDC resorts)
  • Room service late at night (canteens typically close by 10 PM)
  • International cuisine menus
  • Consistent high-speed Wi-Fi at remote locations (Doodhpathri, Gurez)
💡 Tip: JKTDC properties are best treated as a clean, safe, and affordable base — not a luxury experience. The real luxury is the Kashmir landscape outside your window, which no private hotel can price in.

❓ FAQs — JKTDC Huts & Accommodation in Srinagar

How do I book a JKTDC hut in Srinagar?
Book online at jktourism.gov.in, call the TRC Srinagar at 0194-2452691, or visit the Tourist Reception Centre on Boulevard Road in person. Online booking is recommended during peak season. You will need a valid ID proof to complete the booking.
Are JKTDC huts available for foreign tourists?
Yes, foreign tourists can book JKTDC properties. You will need to provide passport details during booking. Some properties near border/strategic areas (like Gurez) require an Inner Line Permit (ILP) for foreign nationals — check requirements before booking. The TRC Srinagar staff can assist with ILP information.
Is there a government tourist bungalow on Dal Lake in Srinagar?
Yes. JKTDC operates tourist accommodation at multiple points along the Dal Lake shore in Srinagar — including units within the TRC complex (Boulevard Road, directly opposite Dal Lake), the Nishat Bungalow near Nishat Bagh, and the Cheshmeshahi Bungalow above the lake. The TRC is the most conveniently located for Dal Lake access.
What is the difference between a JKTDC hut and a tourist bungalow?
JKTDC huts (often called log huts or wooden huts) are typically smaller, standalone wooden structures designed for 1–2 occupants — more common at forest locations like Pahalgam. Tourist bungalows are larger, multi-room structures similar to government rest houses, available at most urban and semi-urban destinations including Srinagar. Both are managed by JKTDC and book through the same portal.
Can I walk in without a reservation at JKTDC in Srinagar?
Walk-ins are possible in off-season (November–March) when occupancy is low. During peak season (May–August), walk-in availability is unreliable and often unavailable. Always book in advance, especially for weekends and during the Amarnath Yatra season (July–August) when tourism volume peaks across all Srinagar accommodation.
Does JKTDC offer packages combining huts at multiple locations?
Yes. JKTDC offers multi-destination packages called "Kashmir Circuit Tours" which combine accommodation at JKTDC properties in Srinagar, Gulmarg, Pahalgam, and Sonamarg with transport and local guide services. These are great value for first-time visitors. Details are available at the TRC Srinagar counter or on jktourism.gov.in.
