Pahalgam, known as the "Valley of Shepherds," is a scenic hill station located 95 km from Srinagar in Anantnag district. Situated at an altitude of 2,740 meters, Pahalgam is famous for its lush meadows, pine forests, the Lidder River, and as the base camp for the Amarnath Yatra.

Best Time to Visit Pahalgam

Summer (April-June): Perfect weather (10-25°C) for trekking, sightseeing, and river rafting. Peak tourist season.

Monsoon (July-August): Moderate rain, fewer tourists. Amarnath Yatra happens during this period.

Autumn (Sep-Nov): Pleasant weather, golden meadows, less crowded.

Winter (Dec-Mar): Heavy snowfall, freezing temperatures (-5 to 5°C). Ideal for snow lovers but many roads close.

🎿 Best For: Summer for trekking and activities. Winter for snow and solitude. Avoid late Dec-Jan if you're not prepared for extreme cold.

Top Places to Visit in Pahalgam

1. Betaab Valley (15 km from Pahalgam)

Named after the Bollywood film "Betaab," this stunning valley is surrounded by snow-capped peaks and lush meadows. Perfect for picnics, photography, and horse riding.

Entry Fee: ₹20 per person
Pony Ride: ₹600-1,000
Best Time: April-October

2. Aru Valley (12 km from Pahalgam)

A pristine village known as the base for treks to Kolahoi Glacier and Tarsar Marsar lakes. Aru offers camping, fishing in Aru River, and breathtaking mountain views.

Activities: Trekking, camping, horse riding, fishing
How to Reach: Taxi ₹600-800, or shared jeep ₹100-150 per person

3. Chandanwari (16 km from Pahalgam)

Starting point of Amarnath Yatra and a popular snow point. Even in summer, you can find snow patches here. Sledging and snow play available year-round.

Taxi Fare: ₹1,500-2,000 round trip
Union Cab (Shared): ₹300-400 per person

4. Baisaran Valley (5 km from Pahalgam)

Known as "Mini Switzerland," Baisaran is a high-altitude meadow offering panoramic views of snow-capped peaks. Accessible only by pony or trekking.

Pony Ride: ₹800-1,200 return
Trek Duration: 45 minutes one-way

5. Lidder River

Flowing through Pahalgam, Lidder River is ideal for trout fishing, river rafting (seasonal), and riverside picnics. The sound of gushing water and pine-scented breeze make it perfect for relaxation.

Things to Do in Pahalgam

  1. Trekking: Kolahoi Glacier trek (3 days), Tarsar Marsar trek (6 days), Lidderwat trek
  2. River Rafting: April-June on Lidder River (₹1,000-1,500 per person)
  3. Trout Fishing: Permits available from J&K Tourism office
  4. Horse Riding: Explore valleys on ponies (₹600-1,500 depending on distance)
  5. Golfing: 18-hole golf course at Pahalgam Golf Club
  6. Photography: Meadows, pine forests, and mountain backdrops

How to Reach Pahalgam

From Srinagar (95 km, 2.5 hours)

Taxi: ₹3,000-4,000 (full day with sightseeing)
Shared Taxi: ₹400-600 per seat
Bus: JKSRTC buses ₹150-200 per person

Via Srinagar Airport

Srinagar Airport is 105 km from Pahalgam. Direct taxi ₹3,500-4,500.

Via Jammu (300 km, 9 hours)

Jammu Tawi Railway is nearest station. Hire taxi (₹7,000-9,000) or bus to Srinagar, then onward to Pahalgam.

Where to Stay in Pahalgam

Luxury Hotels: ₹6,000-15,000/night (WelcomHotel, Hotel Heevan Pahalgam)
Mid-Range: ₹2,000-5,000/night (Hotel Paradise, Pine Palace)
Budget Guesthouses: ₹800-1,800/night
Camping: ₹1,500-3,000/night (Aru Valley, Betaab Valley)

Pahalgam Itinerary (2 Days)

Day 1: Arrive Srinagar, drive to Pahalgam (2.5 hrs). Visit Betaab Valley, Aru Valley. Evening walk by Lidder River. Overnight Pahalgam.

Day 2: Morning visit Baisaran Valley (pony ride). Afternoon Chandanwari. Return Srinagar evening.

Travel Tips

  • Carry Cash: Limited ATMs in Pahalgam. Withdraw in Srinagar.
  • Warm Clothes: Even in summer, evenings are cold (10°C). Carry jacket.
  • Permits: Fishing permits from Tourism office. No special entry permits for Indian tourists.
  • Altitude: Drink water, avoid alcohol first day to acclimatize.
